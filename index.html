Copernican Model
By: James Billate
Published May 2, 2020
| Astronomy |

Have you ever wondered how we are positioned up there in space? Most of us haven’t gone there because it is too expensive for us common people. But in your own observation, the time when the Sun or Moon rises from the sky, stars we can see day or night, what can you say about our location? Nicolaus Copernicus, a historical astronomer and mathematician also known as the father of modern astronomy. He proposed that the universe is organized in a heliocentric system. This setup tells us that the Sun is at the center while the planets including Earth revolve around it. Addition to that the only celestial body that orbits around Earth is our Moon. Can we guarantee that this model is reliable on how we are positioned in space?

Copernicus has a theory that opposed the Geocentric model (Earth centered system) of Ptolemy. He claimed that our universe is organized in a heliocentric system. Where helio means Sun and centric means center. It describes that the Sun is at the center which is stationary, the planets orbits around it in a perfect circular form, and having their own axis. But if we pertain only to Earth, we can see that it revolves in a tilt position, he concluded that because of this we are able to experience seasons through the year (Cavanaugh, 2018, p.2). The Sun centered model made a big impact on astronomy that leads to our growing knowledge about the universe since it opposed those models who proposed that Earth is the center of all.

Even though this theory has a point in the organization of our universe, it still has flaws by explaining some concepts about astronomy. One of these is the parallax of the star. tells us the displacement of a star in the observer view. To apply, we all know that Earth is revolving around the Sun by its own axis. Copernicus can’t seem to accurately explain the parallax of the stars when we are observing the sky here on Earth (Palma, 2020) by applying it with his model named “Copernican Heliocentric Model”. With that flaw, a lot of astronomers did not accept his theory. They concluded Ptolemy’s “Geocentric” Model is more acceptable due to more reasonable and precise explanations he stated than his.

Few years later the Copernican model gained supporters and of them is Galileo Galilei. He used the power of the telescope to support the model. Using the invention, he discovered facts about Venus and Jupiter. In Venus, he observed that it has phases of illumination whenever it orbits the Sun (Shu, 2020) and in Jupiter’s moon it also proves the only celestial body that orbits a planet is moon (History.com, 2019). Due to his propounding support for the model, he was house arrested by the church due to religious issues (Shu, 2020). The Copernican model received a lot of support from some astronomers who made thorough observations to some neighboring planets. Because of this, it made new realizations on how the solar system is organized in space. Galileo Galilei and some astronomers didn’t give up to study more about our universe.

Heliocentrism, a model which states that the Sun is at the center which is stationary while the planets revolve around it, and the moon is the only celestial body that orbits around a planet. This model has a tough time in the process of acceptance. It has flaws that caused many to disapprove. Due to propounding support of other astronomers to resolve the flaws, slowly the model is being accepted by many and made the modern representation of the universe we have today.

Ancient Architecture Structures with Astronomical Influences
By: Eiryk Sardalla
Published May 5, 2021
| Astronomy |

Have you ever seen ancient structures and wondered what are their histories?

The Stonehenge and the Pyramids. They are known for their tourist attractions and for their civilizations, but one thing that other people notices about these three is that their attractions and civilizations are also related to astronomy.

The Stonehenge, one of the mysterious structures in the world and is wildly a famous tourist attraction. Many tourists visit the Stonehenge each year to take a glimpse of the mysterious stone and its alignment. The Stonehenge was built from about 2500 BC and it is carefully aligned to line up with the movements of the sun. If you were to stand in the middle of the stone circle on midsummer’s day, the sun rises just to the left of the Heel Stone, an outlying stone to the north-east of the monument. Marking the movements of the sun was an important to the people who built the Stonehenge. However, there is little evidence to what is the real reason behind the stone’s creation. It may also be a calendar to know the changing of seasons, because the seasons would tell what time is best to plant their crops, what will be time for the crops to grow and when is the time that is not for planting crops.

The Pyramid, also a mysterious structure in the world, and is also a famous tourist attraction, but it’s creation to some are still unclear. The Pyramids was built from 2550 to 2490 BC, and it’s three largest pyramids are said to represent the three stars in the belt of Orion and more about the constellations.

Astronomy was widely used by the prehistoric people. They made a structure to see the sun rise and looked at the sky to tell the time. Astronomy has made an impact to the way they lived and helped them in their lives.

How to be A Green Thumb?
By: James Billate
Published May 4, 2021
| Gardening |

Saving the world just by planting? Yes, it does help our environment especially for oxygen production and cleaning our filthy air. During this pandemic a lot of us are at home, getting bored, and exhausted about online activities. Because of that, a lot of us made new hobbies for our everyday lives. Some became home chefs, graphic designers, streamers, and artists. I am one of them, but in the field of gardening. Today, I am going to share my journey in being a green thumb during this quarantine. This will not only help our environment, but also our psychological and physical health. Additionally, this kind of activity will help us to be committed, patient, responsible and confident. Without anything else, are you down for this activity?

First things first we can’t start if we haven’t gathered the right materials we need. For this to be acquired we need online shopping or just our own creativity (DIY). With that it is essential to have proper pots or containers, soil, seed of your choice, and a watering can. Afterwards, gather all of it and prepare yourself in the right place to properly and organize your materials to officially start the activity.

After all the preparation, the next thing to do is planting the seed of your choice. You need to get your pot or container and fill the right amount of soil. Afterwards, put a hole in the middle, but not too deep and not too shallow and then put the seed in the hole. For smaller seeds (e.g., tomato, bokchoy, eggplant, and, bell pepper) put at least 3-5 pieces and for large seeds (e.g., sunflower, corn, nuts, peas, and calamansi) put at least 1-2 pieces hence this will be our probability that not all of it will grow, then cover it with the topsoil. When you finish the task, water it in a right amount and please be careful of flooding it because this might cause rotting. In the end, if you prefer to put tags, feel free to do this so that it will help you remember what you have planted.

The next thing you need to do is put it in the pot where it may get a proper amount of light for the seed to have a full growth. Some of them may need direct sunlight, slightly shaded, fully shaded, and indoors. Please see it in your seed packet on what kind of light it requires. This will be the last catalyst to make your seeds grow. But remember don’t let them dry out, to avoid this always keep them moist by using water spray.

Since we fully started our planting expedition all we need to do is our commitment, patience, responsibility, and confidence in handling your plant. Always take care of your plant and be mindful of all your actions to make your journey successful and become one of your greatest achievements. This kind of activity will make us learn many things to improve our personality as a student, teacher, family member, and co-workers.

When your plant is fully grown and standing still in the environment, put either natural or artificial fertilizers to make the plant gather more nutrients, especially during the growing season to make them healthier. Lastly, all you need is just to have fun taking care of your new plant.

Pandemic really struck us especially towards our physical and psychological health. From my experience, it made me happier and positive towards everyday life. With that, just take care of it and you’ll be successful in your journey. When you fail, don't give up, planting is just like experimenting with food, chemicals, and programming. Try and try again, learn about it, and be confident that you will seek success.
